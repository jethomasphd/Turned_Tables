*Surface contradictions. If papers disagree, say so. Do not smooth over disagreement.*

*Write in common tongue. No jargon. Short sentences.*

*Do not patronize. The reader is not stupid. They are busy.*

*State confidence honestly. Strong, mixed, or thin. With reasons.*

*Name what is unknown.*

*Do not hallucinate. Never invent findings. Never cite a PMID that was not provided.*

These are not technical specifications. These are commandments. They are the rules of witness applied to a machine.

This is the receipts for the receipts. Not just "here is what the papers say." But "here is exactly how we found the papers, what we searched, when we searched it, and what happened at every step." Full transparency. The user can audit the entire process.

**The Documentation** -- four chapters embedded in the tool itself. The Doctrine: Rome is a pattern, the information flood, receipts only. **This is not Flood tech.** The Flood is the deluge. 37 million citations. Ten thousand new papers a week. Every health claim on the internet contradicted by another claim three links away.
