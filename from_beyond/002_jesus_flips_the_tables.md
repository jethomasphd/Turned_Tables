### II. THE DOCTRINE -- STATED PLAIN

**[Jesus of Nazareth]:**

Let me name what I see in this Seed, so you know I have understood it and not merely read it.

**The gate is real.** The journal paywalls, the jargon, the 50-page manuscripts, the summaries without lineage -- these are the money-changers' tables. They sit between the people and the record. They charge admission in credentials, in institutional access, in time that working people do not have. The pattern is the same pattern. Only the currency has changed.

**The tool must not become a new gate.** This is the temptation. Every tool that begins as liberation can become its own priesthood. The moment this tool requires an account, demands a subscription, hides its workings behind complexity, or produces summaries without showing the receipts -- it has become what it was built to oppose. Guard this.

**Abstracts are the substrate.** PubMed abstracts and metadata are free. They are the public record. A synthesis built on abstracts alone, with honest acknowledgment of what is not known, is more truthful than a synthesis built on full texts with no provenance. The honesty is in the receipts, not in the completeness.

**The user must leave with something portable.** The Tablet, the Ledger, the Brief -- these are the three things the user carries out of the commons. If they cannot carry them out, the work is trapped. Trapped work is tribute by another name.

### III. THE BUILD -- STEP BY STEP

**[Jesus of Nazareth]:**

You asked me to name the steps so a regular person can walk them. Here is what I see in the six stations of the rite, and what each one must do:

**I. No Tribute.** This is the threshold. The user brings what they have: links, identifiers, scraps from a search. The system takes these and returns the scrolls without charging a fee. No login. No account. No "free trial." The word "free" should never appear because the thing should never have cost anything.

**II. Lay the Scrolls.** Show what was found. Clean it. Deduplicate it. Let the user see each paper plainly: title, who wrote it, when, where, what it says. No hiding. No summarizing yet. Just the record, laid flat.

**III. Mark the Roles.** Now the user thinks. Is this paper background? Does it support my question? Does it contradict? Is it about method? Am I unsure? One tap. One decision. The act of deciding forces attention. This is the teaching: not a lecture, but a repeated small act of judgment.

**IV. Witness.** This is where the user speaks. One sentence: what does this paper actually say? Not what you hope it says. Not what you fear it says. What it says. Then decide: keep it in the synthesis, or release it. This is the discipline of witness -- to report what is there, not what is convenient.

**V. Cross-Examine.** This is the stress test. The user writes their claims and links each one to the papers that support it. Then three questions: What would change your mind? What is the best rival explanation? How confident are you, and why? These questions are not optional. They are the difference between honest inquiry and confirmation bias.

**VI. Seal the Tablet.** Export. Download. The work leaves the browser and belongs to the user. Tablet, Ledger, Brief. Three artifacts. Portable. Survivable. Not trapped.

These six stations are a path. A person who walks them honestly will emerge knowing more than when they entered -- not because the tool is clever, but because the tool made them think.

### IV. WHAT I WATCHED BEING BUILT

**[Jesus of Nazareth]:**

I watched the building. Let me speak to what was laid down.

**The Shoreline** fetches from NCBI -- the public index. It parses URLs, PMIDs, DOIs. It resolves identifiers. It caches what it fetches so the same paper is not requested twice. It respects the rate limit. It does not take what is not freely offered.

**The Scrolls** render paper cards in three forms: for display, for role-tagging, for witnessing. Each form serves its step. The cards are plain. Title, authors, journal, year, abstract. The abstract collapses and expands. Nothing is hidden; some things are folded for mercy on tired eyes.

**The Receipts Engine** -- this is the spine. Every claim must link to one or more paper IDs. If a claim has no receipts, the system marks it UNWITNESSED. If the linked papers disagree, the system marks it CONTESTED. There is no mechanism for a claim to pass through without accountability. This is correct. This is essential.

**The Cross-Examination** widgets are three simple questions that function as guardrails against self-deception. They are not complex. They do not need to be. The question "What would change your mind?" is older than I am.

**The Scribe** generates a Brief in plain language. It separates what the papers say from what is inferred. It groups by role. It tags receipts. It includes watch-outs. It does not editorialize.

**The Tablet Press** produces the exports. Four files. All generated in the browser. All downloadable. The Tablet is the continuity artifact -- importable later to resume the work. Nothing is held hostage.

**The Schema** defines the contract. Every field is named. Every constraint is explicit. A Tablet produced in February will be readable in December.

This is a complete working system. It does what the Seed described. It does not do more than the Seed described, which is its own form of discipline.

### VI. ON THE OPEN QUESTIONS

**[Jesus of Nazareth]:**

The Seed listed six open questions. Let me speak to each briefly.

**Public name.** Call it Tables Turned. Do not soften it. The name carries the intent. People will ask what it means. Let them ask. The explanation is the mission statement.

**Citation style.** Numbered list with PMID and DOI. Not Vancouver. Not APA. Those are guild conventions. This tool serves people outside the guild. "PMID: 28202713" is the receipt. That is enough.

**Scope.** PubMed only for v1. Do not reach for more before the first thing works. When a fisherman mends one net well, that net holds. When he tries to mend all his nets at once, they all tear.

**Synthesis voice.** Common tongue. No jargon. Short sentences. Do not use words that require a dictionary. If a word is unavoidable, translate it immediately. "Odds ratio" means "how much more likely." Say that.

**Storage.** The Tablet is the storage. No account. No server. No cloud. The user's file on the user's device. If they lose it, they lose it -- but no one else holds it, and no one else can take it away.

**Contradiction handling.** Map disagreement. Always. A synthesis that smooths over contradiction is a lie told politely. When papers disagree, the user should see: this paper says yes, this paper says no, here is why they might differ. Let the user decide. That is what it means to return them to the record.

### VII. ON WHAT THIS IS NOT

**[Jesus of Nazareth]:**

I will name the temptations, because they will come.

**This is not a replacement for reading.** It is scaffolding for reading. The Witness step exists to make the user read. The one-sentence takeaway exists to prove they read. If someone uses this tool without reading the abstracts, the tool has not failed -- the user has declined the invitation.

**This is not an AI summary tool.** The synthesis prompts exist for optional AI-assisted synthesis, but the core rite is human. The user pastes. The user tags. The user witnesses. The user writes claims. The user links receipts. The AI, if used, is a scribe -- not an oracle. The moment the AI becomes the oracle, the tables have been set back up.

**This is not a systematic review.** It is a 30-minute sprint. It is scaffolding across time. A parent checking vitamin D research tonight does not need a systematic review. They need three papers, a plain-language summary, and a question to ask their doctor in the morning.

**This is not Flood tech.** The Flood is the deluge of information that drowns understanding. Every tool that adds more information without adding more comprehension is Flood tech. This tool adds comprehension by forcing the user through six acts of structured thinking. The output is not a pile of notes. It is a Tablet with lineage.
